Daniel Byrne Activity 1: Basic HTML Bio

"Daniel Byrne is a graduate student in the Division of the Social Sciences at the University of Chicago with a focus in Comparative Human Development. Daniel received his Bachelor of Science Summa Cum Laude PBK in Cognitive Psychology and Communication Science from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2016. He currently serves as the Co-Investigator of the FARSA-2 project for the University of Chicago Learning Lab along side Dr. Lindsey Richland. His prior research & professional experience include working as a research assistant for the University of Chicago Medicine's Thirty Million Words Initiative, the University of Illinois Human Attention Laboratory, a U.S. Department of Defense project on military couples & families post-deployment, & a law clerkship with the Felony Trial Division of the Cook County State's Attorney's Office. Daniel's aptitude for research also translates into a natural enthusiasm for instruction & teaching. He possesses experience as a teaching assistant for traditional lecture style courses, educational facilitations, & as the independent instructor of record for an introductory freshmen seminar course in the liberal arts & sciences. These twin passions for research & teaching dovetailed into several honors & awards including but not limited to: the prestigious James D. and Dorothy C. Di Iorio Scholarship, the Charles Eriksen Award for Excellence in Scholarship and Research in Visual Cognition & Human Performance, & the 2016 University of Illinois College of Liberal Arts and Sciences Teaching Excellence Award--in recognition of Daniel's instructor and course evaluations for earning a perfect 5.0/5.0 in Overall Teaching Effectiveness on every single student evaluation. This diverse background formed the backdrop & provided the basis for his current research interests in the social cognitive processes implicated in socialization ideologies, analogical reasoning, interpersonal communication as a narration, cognition, & cultural paradigm, mediated identity, involved interviewing, intersubjectivity, content analysis, & most broadly language, learning, & the emergence of meaning."
